# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences, one surface:

- **Calon pengantin (couples)** planning their own wedding — self-serve, price-sensitive, want to see a concrete visual before committing budget to a vendor. Often need to reconcile personal taste with family/adat expectations (Jawa, Sunda, national styles).
- **Wedding decoration professionals** (freelance decorators, small decoration studios/vendors) — use it to turn a client's vague brief into a visual fast, for pitching or aligning before physical execution.

Both groups share the same job: turn a typed description into a believable decoration visual in seconds, without hiring a designer or booking a vendor first.

## Product Purpose

Decora AI is a self-serve AI decoration-design tool: type a wish (style, culture, colors, budget) → get an AI-generated wedding decoration image in seconds → optionally run a decoration-checklist analysis for vendor negotiation → optionally upgrade to a human team for production-ready, vendor-ready assets.

The landing page's job is narrow and specific: get a visitor from "curious" to "typed something and saw it become an image" as fast as possible. Everything else (pricing, testimonials, FAQ, how-it-works) exists to remove hesitation before or after that first generate, not to replace it. Success on the landing page is measured by first-generate rate, not scroll depth.

Monetization is hybrid and sequential: free generation (capped, watermarked) → Plus subscription (unlimited-ish, HD, no watermark) → per-project human-team finishing (vector, culturally-curated, production-ready). The landing page primarily sells the free entry point; Plus and Team are upgrade paths surfaced, not the hard sell.

## Brand Personality

**Warm craftsman berbudaya** — this reads like a skilled local decorator who genuinely understands adat detail (gebyok, paes ageng, janur), not a generic AI image tool that happens to accept Indonesian words. Three words: *heritage-literate, tactile, quietly confident.*

- Confident without being loud — the product works, it doesn't need to oversell with hype language or gradient-metric theatrics.
- Warmth comes from specificity (naming real adat elements correctly, Indonesian-first copy, a single restrained clay accent) — not from decoration, cream-on-cream softness, or cutesy copy.
- Self-serve is the hero; the human-team option is a respected upgrade, never a "our AI isn't good enough, hire a real person" undertone.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS template** — gradient blobs, stock-icon grids, hero-metric-card cliché (big number / small label / gradient accent), tiny uppercase eyebrows above every section. Reads as "assembled," not "made for weddings."
- **Situs wedding murahan** — script fonts stacked everywhere, excessive floral flourish, cheesy stock couple photography, rose-gold-on-white overload. Reads as a template marketplace freebie, undermines the "premium craftsman" positioning.
- **Kaku & korporat** — over-minimal, cold, enterprise-SaaS restraint that strips out the romance/occasion entirely. The product must stay emotionally warm even while being efficient and confident.

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't explain.** The entire pitch is "type it, watch it become an image in seconds" — the landing page must let a visitor *do that*, above the fold, before reading marketing copy about it. A screenshot of the feature is weaker than the feature itself being reachable in one action.
2. **Culturally fluent, not generically warm.** Warmth is earned by correctly naming and honoring specific Indonesian wedding traditions (gebyok, paes ageng, janur, Jawa × modern, Sunda), not by generic cream palettes or soft-focus stock imagery. When in doubt, be specific over vibey.
3. **One accent, spent deliberately.** The existing clay-on-paper system with a single restrained accent color stays; craftsman warmth is carried by typography, copy, and cultural specificity — not by adding more color or ornament.
4. **Self-serve first, team as upgrade — never a downgrade.** Every surface should present AI generation as the complete, credible primary path. The human-team tier is framed as "for production-ready polish," never as "because the AI result wasn't good enough."
5. **Earn trust through concreteness.** This audience has been burned by generic tools that "don't nyambung" with local styles. Prefer concrete, checkable claims (named adat terms, specific step counts, real trade-offs) over vague superlatives ("amazing," "revolutionary").

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA baseline: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast (large/bold text ≥3:1), full keyboard operability on nav/forms/accordion/dialogs, visible focus states, and respect for `prefers-reduced-motion` (partially implemented in `index.css` already — extend this coverage rather than reinventing it). No additional stated accessibility requirements beyond standard AA; audience skew is general consumer (couples) plus small-business vendors, not a specialized-needs population.
